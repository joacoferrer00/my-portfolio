# F1 Data Analysis — Historical Driver Performance

An academic project combining data engineering, SQL analysis, and Power BI visualization on a Formula 1 dataset covering all races through the 2023 Bahrain GP.

## Dataset

Kaggle Formula 1 Drivers Dataset — 541,000+ rows covering race results, driver stats, and championship data from the beginning of F1 history.

## Process

### Data Engineering (Python)
- Cleaned and normalized 541k+ rows with Pandas
- Handled missing values, type mismatches, and inconsistent naming

### SQL Analysis
- Queries for top drivers by wins, championships, and nationality
- Identified career length distributions and cross-era comparisons

### Exploratory Analysis (Python)
- Distribution of years active: most drivers have 1 year or fewer — the sport is highly selective
- Near-linear relationship between years active and total races
- Strong correlation between poles, wins, podiums, and points — consistency drives success

### Power BI Dashboard
- Interactive dashboard with filters by driver and nationality
- Visualizations: career timelines, points progression, nationality breakdown

## Key Insights
- Hamilton is the statistical GOAT across all modern metrics
- Strong correlation between qualifying poles and race wins
- Career longevity is the exception — most drivers never complete a full season
- Only a small elite group achieves multi-season sustained success

## Skills Applied
Python data cleaning, SQL analytical queries, EDA with visualization libraries, Power BI dashboard design.
